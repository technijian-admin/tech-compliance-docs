WISP-001
One-time + Annual · Portal #1459229		 Deploy SPF Records for All Domains
SPF (Sender Policy Framework) is a DNS record that tells the world which mail servers are authorized to send email from our domain. Without it, spammers can send email that looks like it came from @technijian.com — this is called email spoofing. Publish SPF TXT records in DNS for every Technijian domain with a -all hard-fail policy. Verify using MXToolbox. Document all authorized senders (M365, marketing platforms, ticketing system). Verify annually that it's still accurate.
WISP-002
One-time + Annual key rotation · Portal #1459231		 Configure DKIM Signing for Outbound Email
DKIM (DomainKeys Identified Mail) adds a cryptographic signature to every outbound email so the receiving server can verify the email truly came from us and wasn't tampered with in transit. Enable DKIM in Microsoft 365 for all sending domains. Generate 2048-bit DKIM keys, publish the CNAME records in DNS, verify signatures are active by examining email headers. Rotate keys annually.
WISP-003
One-time + Monthly · Portal #1459232		 Implement DMARC Policy at Reject
DMARC (Domain-based Message Authentication) sits on top of SPF and DKIM. It tells receiving servers what to do when an email fails those checks — nothing (p=none), quarantine it, or reject it outright. Start at p=none so you can see what's happening via aggregate reports (rua) without breaking anything, advance to p=quarantine after 30 days, then p=reject after 60. Monitor the weekly DMARC reports — they'll show you if any legitimate senders are failing authentication that you need to fix first.

B4 — CMMC / NIST 800-171 — Technical Implementation

CMMC (Cybersecurity Maturity Model Certification) and NIST SP 800-171 are federal standards for protecting Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI) — sensitive government data. We are pursuing DoD-adjacent work, which means clients working with the Department of Defense. To serve those clients, we need to demonstrate we can handle their security posture without putting their CUI at risk. Critically: Technijian does not store, process, or transmit CUI on our own systems. Our model is ciphertext-only — encrypted backups where the client holds the key and we physically cannot decrypt the data. Every tool we use must preserve that architecture.

CMMC-002
One-time + Annual · Portal #1459284		 Conduct NIST 800-171 Self-Assessment and Calculate SPRS Score
NIST SP 800-171 has 110 security requirements. The SPRS (Supplier Performance Risk System) score is how the DoD measures how well a contractor implements those 110 controls — starting at 110, with points deducted for unimplemented controls. You perform the self-assessment using the DoD Assessment Methodology: for each of the 110 controls, determine met or not met, document evidence, and apply the weighted scoring. The resulting SPRS score gets submitted to the DoD. Re-score after any material remediation.

Verify and document that all cryptography protecting CUI uses FIPS-validated modules: enable BitLocker FIPS mode on CUI-handling endpoints, confirm FIPS-validated VPN/TLS for transmission, record the CMVP certificate numbers.
